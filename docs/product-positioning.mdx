# Product positioning

## The problem BNII solves

The intelligence industry — consulting, market research, credit bureaus, panel measurement — sees the top and middle of markets well, and the bottom of emerging markets barely at all. Hundreds of millions of prepaid telco users, first-time wallet holders, and cash-adjacent consumers across Indonesia, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Ethiopia, Rwanda, and Nigeria remain close to invisible to people who sell insight for a living.

Because we are embedded inside the apps those consumers already use — as their telco, wallet, or bank — we generate **consented behavioural signal at the point of action**. BNII is the layer that turns that position into sellable, market-level intelligence.

## Why now

| Fact | Implication |
| --- | --- |
| ~384M monthly transacting users across the partner footprint | The position is live today |
| Frontier models are rentable commodities | Models are no longer the scarce asset |
| Consent + embedded footprint is hard to replicate market-by-market | Window is open; first-mover compound advantage |

## Aria's role

Aria is the **human-facing intelligence surface**: institutional briefs, cited answers, and eventually metered queries against governed datasets. It is not the data plane. It consumes (and will later meter) intelligence that BNII produces.

```
Partner Apps → Event Pipeline → Consent Layer → Classification Engine → Governed Data Lake
                                                                              ├→ Intelligence APIs
                                                                              ├→ AI Query Engine (Aria)
                                                                              ├→ Customer Models
                                                                              └→ Execution Environment
```

## Five commercial surfaces — one asset

Every surface sits behind the **same consent gates**, the **same k-floor**, and the **same audit-and-metering ledger**. Governance does not weaken as depth increases — it is what makes depth sellable.

| # | Surface | Depth | Buyer intent |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Credits** | Shallow | Metered Q&A / briefs — audit log = billing ledger |
| 2 | **Build on top** | Medium | Use our intelligence as substrate for their models |
| 3 | **Bring your own data** | Medium–deep | Their data × our market backdrop |
| 4 | **Data & intelligence APIs** | Deep | Pull indices / structured intel into their pipelines |
| 5 | **Run on our infra** | Deepest | Model comes to data; raw signal never egresses |

Aria today primarily serves surface **1** (and the UX for briefs). Surfaces 2–5 are platform roadmap — see [BNII surfaces](/docs#bnii-surfaces).
